About the Laboratory

The NVO987 Research Laboratory is a research-in-progress environment dedicated to the exploration of visual culture, computational aesthetics, semantic systems, artificial intelligence, and digital knowledge infrastructures.

Unlike the Knowledge Infrastructure, which preserves validated publications, datasets, and archival resources, the Laboratory documents research while it is being developed. It provides a transparent environment for theoretical notes, exploratory investigations, intermediate findings, experimental methodologies, and conceptual development.

The objective is not only to publish conclusions, but also to preserve the intellectual process that leads to those conclusions.

Research Workflow

Research within the laboratory follows a progressive workflow from exploration to long-term preservation.

01

Research Notes

Initial observations, theoretical questions, reading notes, and conceptual fragments.

02

Experiments

Visual studies, AI-assisted exploration, methodological testing, and computational investigations.

03

Analysis

Interpretation, synthesis, comparative study, and theoretical refinement.

04

Knowledge Infrastructure

Mature outputs are transferred to the Knowledge Infrastructure for long-term preservation and machine-readable access.
